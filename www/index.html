DART 335

Interpretive Public Spaces

Assignment 1: Pocket Place Concordia

Program

  • Green (planted) surfaces must occupy 25-50% of overal surface area
  • A tree (at least)
  • Between 5-10 places to sit
  • A paving pattern based on a module
  • A light or lights
  • An interpretive device that refers to either history of neighbourhood, St. Jax, Concordia, or sustainability in general.

Chosen Site: Site "B"

Context plan, scale: 1:500
Rough site dimensions, scale: 1:50

Proposition 2

Bring in the sun!

Overview

Space does not get much sun. So that people can enjoy the area, a structure could be built to capture the sun and reflect it into the area.

Proposition 3

Nec plus ultra: A S.A.T.-like active area

Overview

Permanent inter/active environment
where Concordia students or guests
could present works.

  • Visual projection on screen or walls of building
  • Quadraphonic sound textures
    (requires addition of structure to hold 4th speaker)
  • Possibility of adding motion sensors
    (investigate use of camera vs. privacy)

Open to student use

Public installation of interactive arts
is quite expensive, or impossible
(try getting featured at the S.A.T.)

Students of IMCA, Computation Arts, Design and Electro Acoustic could use environment.
Not restricted to those fields, but most likely candidates.

Visual projections

There are screens used in theater that allow to see through when there is light from behind, and to project onto when desired.

Quadraphonic sound

Addition of a structure at south-east end of space allows to create a room-shaped area, which would be perfect for confined sound diffusion.

St. Crickets in the Fields

While nickname was originally given because British armymen were playing cricket in the fields around St. Jax, it could be inspiring to use the nickname a bit more litteraly to create a piece to present in the space.

Nature sounds and greenery could be generated depending on the movement,
or lackthereof, in the space.

Common ideas

Tile modules

Straight Lines

Inspired by Passeig de Sant Joan in Barcelona,
this simple design allows to have
an equal amount of greenery and floor.

Tile modules

Hexagrass

Hexagon tiles with a part of grass,
overlaid in a pattern over the surface.

Tile modules

Circular tiles

Circular tiles overlaid over the surface.

Some tiles are not placed,
leaving more space for greenery.

Cedar Hedges

Placing potted cedar hedges near the alley and near the fences of the private properties is a good way to add greenery, as well as to isolate the space to create more of an intimacy.

Non-smoking area

Outside areas and terrasses
are oftentimes overtaken by smokers.

  • This space would be a strictly non-smoking area.
  • This does not mean changing other areas around.

Challenges to resolve

Ground cover

Space does not seem to get much sun,
ground cover would be a better solution than grass.

Space is likely to have lot of foot traffic,
to be taken into account.
